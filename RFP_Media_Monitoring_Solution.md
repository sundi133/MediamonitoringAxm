## 2. Background Information

### 2.1. Basic Information

The **[Issuing Authority / Department Name]** (hereinafter referred to as the "Procuring Entity" or "Authority") is responsible for governance, public communications, and administrative oversight within the jurisdiction. The Authority seeks to modernize its media intelligence capabilities by deploying a state-of-the-art, AI-powered **Media Monitoring & Sentiment Analysis Platform** (hereinafter referred to as the "Platform" or "MMP").

The Platform shall serve as a centralized, real-time media intelligence tool for tracking, analyzing, and reporting on media coverage across digital news portals, print media (digitized), social media channels, broadcast media (transcribed), blogs, forums, and other online information sources. The solution must enable officials and decision-makers to proactively identify emerging issues, gauge public sentiment, track policy reception, and generate data-driven insights for timely intervention and informed governance.

### 2.2. Project Background

In today's rapidly evolving media landscape, government bodies and large organizations require a systematic mechanism to monitor media coverage across an ever-expanding universe of information sources. Traditional manual clipping and monitoring methods are no longer adequate given the volume, velocity, and variety of media content generated daily.

The proliferation of digital news platforms, social media networks, and citizen journalism has fundamentally altered how public discourse unfolds. Issues can escalate within hours, misinformation can spread virally, and public perception can shift rapidly based on media narratives. In this environment, the ability to monitor media in real time and extract actionable intelligence is not merely advantageous—it is essential.

The Authority currently monitors media through a combination of manual processes and basic digital tools that lack the sophistication needed for comprehensive analysis. These limitations include:

- **Delayed Reporting:** Manual monitoring results in a lag of 12–24 hours before critical coverage is identified.
- **Limited Coverage:** Current processes cover only a fraction of digital news sources, with minimal social media monitoring.
- **No Sentiment Intelligence:** There is no systematic mechanism to analyze sentiment trends or gauge public perception.
- **Siloed Information:** Media intelligence is not centralized, leading to fragmented decision-making.
- **Absence of AI/ML Capabilities:** No automated classification, entity extraction, or trend detection exists.
- **Inadequate Reporting:** Reports are generated manually, often lacking depth, visualization, and analytical rigor.

The Authority envisions a comprehensive, AI-driven Media Monitoring Platform that addresses these challenges and provides a unified, real-time view of the media ecosystem.

### 2.3. Objectives of the RFP

The primary objectives of this RFP are to:

1. **Select a qualified and experienced Agency** (hereinafter referred to as the "Bidder" or "Agency") to design, develop, deploy, and maintain an AI-powered Media Monitoring & Sentiment Analysis Platform.

2. **Establish a real-time media monitoring system** capable of tracking mentions across 10,000+ digital news sources, major social media platforms, blogs, forums, and broadcast media (transcribed).

3. **Deploy advanced AI/ML-based sentiment analysis** with accuracy exceeding 85% for multi-lingual content, including regional languages.

4. **Implement intelligent alert and notification systems** that proactively notify stakeholders of critical, negative, or trending media coverage.

5. **Enable automated report generation** with customizable templates, data visualizations, and executive summaries suitable for decision-makers.

6. **Provide an AI-powered conversational interface** enabling natural language queries for media intelligence retrieval and on-demand analysis.

7. **Deliver a scalable, secure, and high-availability platform** with 99.5% uptime SLA, enterprise-grade security, and the ability to handle future expansion.

8. **Ensure comprehensive training and knowledge transfer** to enable the Authority's personnel to independently operate and manage the platform.

b) The Procuring Entity shall not be responsible for any delay in submission of bids due to any reason including but not limited to internet connectivity issues, server downtime, or other technical issues at the Bidder's end.

### 3.8. Evaluation Process

#### 3.8.1. Tender Opening

a) The Pre-Qualification (PQ) Bids shall be opened first on the date and time specified in the Fact Sheet, in the presence of the Bidders or their authorized representatives who choose to attend.

b) The Technical Bids of only those Bidders who qualify in the PQ evaluation shall be opened.

c) The Financial Bids of only those Bidders who score a minimum of **70 marks out of 100** in the Technical Evaluation shall be opened.

d) The date and time of opening of Technical and Financial Bids shall be communicated to the eligible Bidders separately.

#### 3.8.2. Tender Validity

a) The proposal shall be valid for a period of **180 days** from the last date of submission of bids.

b) In exceptional circumstances, the Procuring Entity may request the Bidders' consent for an extension of the period of validity. The request and responses shall be in writing. A Bidder may refuse the request without forfeiting its EMD. A Bidder granting the request will not be required nor permitted to modify its proposal.

#### 3.8.3. Tender Evaluation

a) The evaluation of proposals shall be carried out in **three stages**:

- **Stage 1: Pre-Qualification (PQ) Evaluation** – Evaluation of PQ/Eligibility criteria as specified in Section 4.1.
- **Stage 2: Technical Evaluation** – Evaluation of Technical Proposals of PQ-qualified Bidders as specified in Section 4.2.
- **Stage 3: Financial Evaluation** – Evaluation of Financial Proposals of technically qualified Bidders as specified in Section 4.3.

b) The selection shall be on a **Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS)** basis with the following weightage:

- **Technical Score:** 70%
- **Financial Score:** 30%

c) The **Combined Score** shall be calculated as:

**Combined Score = (Technical Score × 0.70) + (Financial Score × 0.30)**

d) The Bidder with the **highest Combined Score** shall be declared as the successful Bidder (L1).

e) In case of a tie in the Combined Score, the Bidder with the higher Technical Score shall be preferred. If the Technical Score is also the same, the Bidder with the higher annual turnover (average of last 3 financial years) shall be preferred.

## 5. Scope of Work (SOW)

### 5.1. Overview

The selected Agency shall design, develop, deploy, and maintain an AI-Powered Media Monitoring & Sentiment Analysis Platform that provides comprehensive, real-time media intelligence capabilities. The Platform must be a fully integrated, web-based solution accessible via modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) on desktops, laptops, tablets, and mobile devices.

The Platform shall cover the following broad functional areas:

1. **Multi-Source Media Monitoring** – Real-time tracking across digital news, social media, print (digitized), broadcast (transcribed), blogs, and forums.
2. **AI-Powered Sentiment Analysis** – Automated sentiment classification (positive, negative, neutral) with confidence scoring.
3. **Intelligent Alerting & Notifications** – Proactive, configurable alerts for critical or trending mentions.
4. **Advanced Analytics & Reporting** – Interactive dashboards, trend analysis, custom report generation.
5. **Conversational AI Interface** – Natural language query interface for on-demand media intelligence.
6. **Keyword & Topic Management** – Configurable keyword tracking, topic classification, and entity extraction.
7. **User Management & Access Control** – Role-based access with multi-level permissions.
8. **Data Export & Integration** – APIs and export capabilities for integration with third-party systems.
